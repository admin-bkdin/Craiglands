A Grand Old Soul Since Eighteen Fifty-Nine.

01
The House

Set against the sweeping drama of Ilkley Moor, Craiglands has held its ground since 1859 — sixty-three rooms of Victorian sandstone, bay windows that watch the weather, and a quiet conviction that hospitality is not a service but a small art.

The house has weathered three centuries of moor wind, and not a single one has shifted its mood. — The Yorkshire Post, 1924

Below stairs, Brasserie1 plates the season; above, four-posters and claw-foot baths. The light here is particular — long, low, half-silver — and the welcome older than memory.

— No. 01 / The Brasserie

A kitchen that takes the season at its word. Yorkshire is its larder; the tea trolley its punctuation; an unhurried second pour its quiet creed.

— Brasserie1

A small kitchen, a long memory.

Wensleydale on the cheese board; Whitby crab when the boats are in; hogget from the dale that begins where the lawn ends. The wine list is short, the corkage friendly, and the puddings — to use the proper word — are very good indeed.
